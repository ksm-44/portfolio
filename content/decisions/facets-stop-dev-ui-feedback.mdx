title: "Stopped taking UI feedback from DevOps engineers and developers"
date: "2025-03-10"
status: "validated"
tldr: "The most vocal users were precise about capability and indifferent to usability — so their UI input pulled designs the wrong way."
---

## Hypothesis

Since developers were the users, their interface feedback should be the best guide to the interface.

## Evidence

Their input consistently optimized for power and density over learnability. Watching people who were struggling — rather than the ones with the strongest opinions — surfaced the real usability gaps, and features designed that way got adopted more reliably.

## Result

I separated "what should this do" (ask the experts) from "how should this feel to use" (watch the strugglers). Onboarding friction and workflow abandonment both dropped once UX decisions stopped being driven by the loudest power users.

## What I'd do differently

Nothing major on the call itself, but I'd document the distinction earlier so the team didn't relearn it feature by feature.
