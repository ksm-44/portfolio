## Hypothesis

I went in assuming the technically strongest, most flexible solution would win — more capability should mean more value for engineering users.

## Evidence

Across several platform features, the more sophisticated builds saw slower uptake than simpler alternatives that did less but were obvious to adopt. The pattern held often enough that it stopped looking like coincidence: complexity was a tax users quietly declined to pay.

## Result

I started defaulting to the option that was simplest to learn and adopt, and reserving sophistication for where it demonstrably paid off. Release velocity improved by roughly 30% partly because we stopped over-building.

## What I'd do differently

Trust the adoption signal sooner. I spent effort defending elegant designs that the usage data had already voted against.
