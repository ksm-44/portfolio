## Problem

A PM job search is a multi-round, multi-company campaign over months.
ChatGPT-style chats are amnesiac: ask for Round 2 prep and the model knows
nothing about Round 1's questions, the recruiter's feedback, or where your
mocks scored poorly. You re-paste context every session and it still bleeds
between companies. Prep platforms teach identical frameworks to everyone.
Coaches solve continuity at $150–300/hour.

The job-to-be-done isn't "answer my question." It's "remember my search."

## Constraints

Resume, career history, and mock performance are among the most sensitive
documents a person owns, so cloud-by-default was off the table. Job seekers are
often between paychecks, so recurring AI spend had to stay near zero. Solo build.

## Decision

<DecisionPoint
  question="Cloud models are smarter and faster. Personal data is radioactive. How do you route?"
  options={[
    "Cloud everything — best output quality",
    "Local everything — perfect privacy",
    "Routing layer with a hard rule in code: if sensitive → stay local",
  ]}
  actual={2}
>
  Cloud everything means every mock transcript and resume revision goes to a
  third party; a privacy policy is a promise, not a guarantee. Local everything
  means local 4B/8B models doing heavy synthesis like company research visibly
  worse — and users feel it. The routing layer uses a local 4B for
  classification, a local 8B for interactive mocks, cloud opt-in for
  non-personal synthesis only, plus a "copy assembled prompt" escape hatch to
  use chat subscriptions you already pay for. Effective marginal cost ~$0,
  nothing personal leaves the machine by default, and the quality gap only
  shows where it doesn't hurt.
</DecisionPoint>

Memory is the product. Every application gets an isolated workspace backed by
three-tier memory: SQLite for queryable state, the filesystem for
human-readable artifacts, ChromaDB for semantic retrieval — namespaced per
company so context never bleeds. The payoff is the continuity rule: before any
Round-N prep, the orchestrator loads Round-(N−1) questions, interviewer
feedback, and low-scoring mock categories automatically. That's the behavior of
a coach, and it's impossible in a chat window.

A scope call worth defending: shipped 4 of 6 agents. The wedge was the
continuity loop — the one thing no alternative had — not feature parity with
prep platforms. The reranker, voice mode, and analytics were deferred with a
documented migration path. The assignment coach deliberately refuses to write
finished answers; it guides a 7-step method instead, because a candidate who
submits AI-written work fails the onsite.

## Outcome

Full campaign context per application with zero re-pasting and zero
cross-company bleed. Round-N prep auto-loads prior rounds. Mock scorecards show
per-category weakness trends over time.

What went wrong: local-first has real costs I accepted but underestimated —
mock sessions on the 8B model are noticeably slower than a cloud chat, and two
of the six agents still aren't shipped. The refuse-to-write assignment coach
also feels less magical than a chatbot. I'd make that trade again.

## Learning

Memory is the product, not the model. Continuity beat model quality everywhere
it mattered, and the feedback loop — log real outcomes, and the next round's
prep gets sharper — matters more than any individual feature.
