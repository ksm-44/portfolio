## Problem

Perfios is an AI-powered financial document-intelligence platform. On the platform team, I onboard enterprise use cases — an asset-reconstruction company, lenders, and others — onto the classification and extraction engine. That means domain research, schema configuration, and live demos, then carrying the winning cases all the way into production.

The core difficulty is that the users are financial institutions. Their tolerance for error is close to zero, and a confidently wrong extraction on a financial document is worse than no extraction at all. Every domain also turned out to be its own world: the same principles that worked for one use case did not transfer cleanly to the next, because the documents, the risk, and the operators were different each time.

## Constraints

Security, accuracy, and compliance sit above speed and novelty. Banks and financial institutions adopt new technology — AI especially — far more cautiously than startups, and they prefer stability to being first. That reality shapes every design choice: the product has to earn trust before it earns usage.

## Decision

I leaned into human-in-the-loop rather than fighting it. Across checklist validation, bank onboarding and KYC, and application filling and signing, I architected the policy frameworks and end-to-end customer journeys so that automation handled the volume and humans stayed on the decisions that carried real risk. I delivered four use cases end to end — spanning extraction, fraud, and validation logic plus the UI — which cut manual review effort for credit-operations teams by about half.

I also led prompt engineering and LLM configuration for 20+ enterprise clients, which reduced go-live setup time by roughly 30% and got clients to value faster. For the Mediclaim agent, I diagnosed the turnaround-time bottlenecks with the UX and reworked the HITL flow, projecting a ~20% cut in processing TAT.

<Callout type="insight" title="Context beats frameworks">
  The biggest shift from working in fintech was realizing product principles aren't universal. Unlike consumer products, a bank weights security, accuracy, and stability over speed. Keeping a human on the risky decisions wasn't a limitation to design around — it was the feature that made institutions comfortable enough to adopt at all.
</Callout>

## Outcome

Of 12 enterprise prospects I ran domain research, schema configuration, and demos for, 9 advanced into the active sales pipeline and 3 reached production. I also took the extraction platform's UI/UX from research through design to a shipped release.

The trade-off I accepted: this is deliberately not the fastest way to build. Choosing stability, audit-ability, and human checkpoints means slower, more conservative releases than a consumer product would tolerate. In this market that's the right call — but it's a genuine cost, and naming it honestly is part of the job.

## Learning

Every domain and its users are unique; you can't lift principles from one use case and drop them on another. And in fintech specifically, security and accuracy are the product — get those wrong once and no amount of speed or polish buys the trust back.
