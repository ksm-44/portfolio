## What they did well

The network lock-in is real and earned. Simplify-debts is a quietly brilliant
piece of UX that hides genuine graph math. And the mental model — who owes
whom — never leaks complexity.

## Where it breaks

Capture is still open-app-tap-through-forms friction, which is why entries lag
reality by days. Core convenience features migrated behind Pro. And it's a
silo — the split never reaches your actual ledger, so "my balance is wrong"
persists everywhere else. Building FinBot, shared expenses were the single most
common reason Indian users keep a second app.

## What I'd test next

Chat-native capture ("1200 dinner, split with 3") posting both the expense and
the receivable in one message.

## Verdict

Genius, with caveats. The debt graph is one of the strongest retention
mechanics in consumer fintech, but it protects a product whose capture flow has
barely moved in a decade.
