## What they did well

The two-sided review system remains the best cold-start trust machine ever
shipped. Wishlist-driven browsing turned accommodation into aspirational
content.

## Where it breaks

Drip pricing — the fee and cleaning-charge reveal at checkout optimizes
list-page conversion while training users to expect a bait-and-switch. Host
incentives are misaligned: cleaning fees became a pricing loophole, chore lists
shifted labor to guests. Search ranking increasingly serves monetization over
relevance. The assumption being tested on users: short-term booking completion
outweighs long-term price-trust erosion.

## What I'd test next

Total-price-first ranking as the default (not a toggle), measuring
repeat-booking rate rather than session conversion.

## Verdict

Needs work. The mechanics that built trust — reviews, belonging, host quality —
are being spent down by pricing and ranking choices that favor this quarter's
conversion over next year's repeat bookings.
